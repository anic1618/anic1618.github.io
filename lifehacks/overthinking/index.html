Overthinking

October 7, 2022

Last modified: October 7, 2022

src: https://psyche.co/guides/how-to-stop-overthinking-with-help-from-metacognitive-strategies

Many people who, in trying to find answers or meaning, or in attempting to make the right decision, spend most of their waking hours scrutinising their minds for solutions. Ironically, in this process of trying to figure out how to proceed in life, they come to a standstill.

When we spend too much time analysing our problems and dilemmas, we often end up more at a loss than we were to begin with. On top of that, persistent overthinking can result in a wide range of symptoms such as insomnia, trouble concentrating and loss of energy which, in turn, often leads to further worries regarding one’s symptoms, thereby creating a vicious cycle of overthinking. In some cases, this eventually leads to chronic anxiety or depression.

You might recognise some of them in your own behaviour:

  1. Constantly looking out for threats
  2. Seeking answers and reassurance
  3. Excessive planning
  4. beliefs about thinking that it will help

Solution:

  1. Get to know your trigger thoughts and let them be
  2. Recognise what you can and can’t control
  3. Postpone and reduce your worries and ruminations. Most of the worries are non critical and and can be postponed. Advantages:-
    1. It’s an experiment that challenges the belief that worries and ruminations are uncontrollable. When dedicating themselves to this experiment, most of my clients find that it is indeed possible to postpone worries or ruminations
    2. It’s a way of discovering that trigger thoughts are ephemeral and ever-changing.
    3. way of reducing and containing the amount of time you spend worrying and ruminating
  4. Always solve your worries by writing everything clearly. And courageously This will prevent you from thought cycle and help you to solve problem objectively. Also this will let u know that almost all worries were useless.
  5. Keep yourself occupied. But remember while occupied their should not be any fear.
  6. Think less do more.
